THIRD GENERATION COMPUTERS...!!

  1. The period of third generation was from 1965-1971.
  2. The computers of third generation used Integrated Circuits (IC's) in place of transistors.
  3. A single IC has many transistors, resistors, and capacitors along with the associated circuitry.
  4. The IC was invented by Jack Kilby. This development made computers smaller in size, reliable, and efficient.
  5. In this generation remote processing, time-sharing, multi-programming operating system were used.
  6. High-level languages (FORTRAN-II TO IV, COBOL, PASCAL PL/1, BASIC, ALGOL-68 etc.) were used during this generation.
  7. Eg. IBM-360 series, IBM-370/168, PDP, Honeywell-6000 series, TDC-316, etc.

Advantages :

  • ICs are very small in size as cpmpared to transistors.
    • Also there was Improved performance of these types of computers.
  • Especially, the production cost was cheap as compared to first two generation of computers.

Disadvantages :

  • These computers required air condition as they got heated up quickly.
  • ICs were also sophisticated.
